For this task, I created my profiles on LinkedIn, GitHub, and Kaggle. I chose these platforms because I’ve heard a lot about how important they are for students, especially when it comes to internships and future careers, so I wanted to start early.
While working on LinkedIn, I filled in my education details like my degree, college, and expected graduation year. It felt like I was slowly building my professional identity. On GitHub, I created my profile README where I introduced myself, added my branch and year, and wrote a small line about what I want to learn in the future. It made me feel like I finally have a space to showcase my work, even if I’m just starting. On Kaggle, I explored some datasets and notebooks, which gave me a basic idea of how people actually work with data.
Over the next four years, I plan to keep updating these profiles as I learn new things. I want to upload projects on GitHub, connect with people and explore opportunities through LinkedIn, and improve my skills by practicing more on Kaggle. It’s just a start, but it feels like a good one.
