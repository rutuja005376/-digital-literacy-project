For this task, I created my infographic using Canva. I chose Canva because it’s simple to use and honestly makes designing feel less stressful, even if you’re not very experienced. I wanted my design to look something that my classmates would actually stop and read, not just scroll past.
In the infographic, I focused on explaining digital literacy in a way that feels real and relatable—not just a definition, but something we use every day while studying, searching, or communicating online. I also added a section on useful digital tools that can help students stay organized, improve their work, and learn new things. Along with that, I included some basic but important internet safety tips, like using strong passwords, being careful with links, and protecting personal information.
What I found most interesting was how much difference small things like icons, colors, and layout can make. At first, I kept changing the design because either it looked too plain or too crowded. That was probably the most challenging part—keeping it clean but still informative. Overall, this task made me realize that presenting information in a simple and attractive way actually makes people more likely to understand and remember it
